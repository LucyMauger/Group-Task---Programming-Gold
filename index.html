What is Climate Change?

Climate change is a large scale issue having many visible effects on our society today. It describes a change in the average conditions, such as temperature and rainfall over a long period of time. Some examples include:

  • Pollution
  • Rising sea levels
  • Shrinking mountain glaciers
  • More frequent and more intense drought

What Causes Climate Change?

Many factors contribute to the climate of the Earth, but scientists agree that the Earth has been getting warmer in the past 50 to 100 years. A big part of this has been human activities:

Examples of human activities include:

  • Burning fuel to power factories, cars and buses
  • Cutting down forests
  • Farming livestock

    What Could the Impacts of Climate Change be in the Long-Term?

    • Decrease in sea ice levels
    • An increase in permafrost thawing
    • Changes in precipitation patterns
    • Continued rise in temperatures which are not uniform or smooth

    What Can Individuals Do?

    • Eating less meat and dairy
    • Cut back on flying
    • Don't use the car for short journeys
    • Reduce energy use e.g. turning off lights when you don't need them
